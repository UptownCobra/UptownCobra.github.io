Dry ingredients are essential while baking, but people don't always know why they are used.

Dry Ingredients About Reason to use
White Flour White flour is made by sifting out the germ and bran so that only the endosperm is left. It is the easiest flour to work with and creates great gluten strands Flour is used to create doughs and as a thickening agent
Whole Wheat Flour Whole wheat flour consists of the entire wheat kernal including the germ, bran, and endosperm. It is harder to work with when creating yeast dough because the bran acts as little razors to tear the gluten strands Whole wheat flour is much healthier than white flour.
Baking Soda Baking soda is a essentially a basic mineral. That means that when you add it to something acidic the combination will produce carbon dioxide. Baking soda is used whenever you have an acidic agent and you need to leaven.
Baking Powder Baking powder is a mixture of baking soda and a dry acid that allows you to use baking powder to leaven a recipe without having an acid in the recipe Baking powder is used when there isn't any acidic ingredients
